The second half of the finale, and the thesis made interactive. From the synthetic
observation *alone*, **fit back** the age, distance, extinction, metallicity, and IMF —
then reveal the truth the reader secretly set. A **naive fit** lands precise but
**confidently wrong** (degeneracies, binaries, incompleteness); a **forward-modeled,
differentiable fit** recovers it. Then zoom out: one cluster is one snapshot, so infer a
*population* to recover how clusters evolve.

**Toggles:** naive ↔ forward-model fit · which parameters to solve · one cluster ↔ many.
**Research:** **informax** (inference), fluxax (the forward model), Confidently Wrong.
**Payoff:** how we know any of this.
