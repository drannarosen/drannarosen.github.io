The cluster's death — the "and their clusters" half of the title's promise, and the fate
of the object the reader has followed since Chapter 1.

## Story beat

With the natal gas expelled (Chapter 9) and the massive stars spent, the cluster is a
loosening knot of stars in a galaxy that is pulling on it. **Two-body relaxation** keeps
kicking stars past escape speed (evaporation); the **galactic tide** strips the outskirts.
Most clusters lose this fight and **dissolve into the field** over millions to billions of
years — their stars scattered into the general population. A few, dense enough, survive.

## What the reader does

Let the clock run long; watch stars leak away and the tidal field peel the cluster apart;
compare an isolated cluster to one in a tide, and a top-heavy build to a gentle one — the
choices made back in Chapter 1 decide who survives.

## The research, woven in

- Same **gravax**-ported N-body engine as Chapter 10, now run to disruption; disruption

- The through-line pays off: the α you set at birth shaped the feedback, which shaped the
  binding energy, which shapes whether the cluster lives — one continuous causal chain.

## Physics & data

N-body evaporation + an external tidal field; survival vs dissolution over time.

Field stars, and maybe a bound survivor — the cluster's remains, handed to the finale.
