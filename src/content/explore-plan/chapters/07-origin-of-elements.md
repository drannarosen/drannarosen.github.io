The emotional peak, and the best outreach moment in the whole series: the death you just
watched is where *you* come from.

## Story beat

The universe started with almost nothing but hydrogen and helium. Everything heavier —
the carbon in your cells, the calcium in your bones, the oxygen you breathe, the iron in
your blood — was **forged inside stars and scattered by their deaths**: AGB winds, the
core-collapse supernovae of Chapter 6, neutron-star mergers. A periodic table where every
element is colored by *who made it.*

## What the reader does

Watch each element light up as the cluster's stars live and die; hover an element to see
its origin; step the cluster forward and watch metals accumulate in the gas that will
seed the next generation.

## The research, woven in

- Chains directly from the winds (Ch 5) and deaths (Ch 6) that Anna's stellar work
  models — enrichment is what feedback *carries*, not just energy.
- Rising metallicity closes back onto the series' metallicity toggle (winds, tracks, and
  remnants all shift with Z).

## Physics & data

Illustrative nucleosynthetic-origin fractions per element (standard compilation, cited);
a teaching visualization, not a yield calculation.

## Carries forward

**Enriched gas** — the chemical memory the cluster leaves behind.

## Honesty

Origins are shown as illustrative dominant channels, clearly labeled — not a quantitative
yield model.
