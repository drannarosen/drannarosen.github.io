The reckoning, and the thesis. Everything the reader has done was a god's-eye view no
astronomer ever gets. The finale takes it away and reveals the real problem the whole
field exists to solve.

## Story beat

You set the truth and watched a cluster live. A real astronomer gets none of that — only
**photons**. So the finale hands back only what a telescope delivers: a **mock image**
(blurred, crowded, noisy) and a **reddened color-magnitude diagram**, at a single frozen
instant of a Myr–Gyr life. Now run it backwards: recover age, distance, extinction,
metallicity, and the IMF — and walk straight into the **degeneracies** (age–metallicity,
reddening–distance) that make the answer **confidently wrong**.

Then the twist that reframes the entire series: it's a **snapshot**. A cluster lives for
eons; you get one frame and can never press play. So how does anyone know how clusters are
*born* or *die*? You can't follow one — you **observe thousands, at every age, and stitch
the frozen snapshots into a life.** The series let the reader watch one cluster's whole
life as a gift; reality is the inverse. *That* is how we know all this.

## What the reader does

Synthesize the observation with the **synthetic observatory**; then use the **inference
reckoning** to fit the truth back — naively (confidently wrong) and carefully
(differentiable, forward-modeled → right).

## The research, woven in

- **fluxax** (Sim2SKIRT lineage) turns the true cluster into the corrupted thing a
  telescope sees — dust, PSF, noise, incompleteness.
- **informax** is the inference: recovering parameters from observations is *the* task.
- **Confidently Wrong** is now one instance of the general dilemma.

## Physics & data

fluxax-style synthetic image + reddened CMD; a naive-vs-forward-model IMF/age fit with
visible degeneracies; a "observe many clusters" population view.

## The ending

The reader leaves knowing astrophysics is not a list of facts but an act of **inference
from incomplete light** — powerful, humbling, and exactly what Anna's computational
program is for.
