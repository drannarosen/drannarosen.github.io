Still following one star — and now it starts to come apart on the wind. This is home
turf for Anna's research.

## Story beat

A hot, luminous star drives a **line-driven wind**: radiation pushes on metal ions and
blows a steady gale off the surface. Over its life a massive star can shed a large
fraction of its mass this way — mass that will *never* end up in the remnant, and that
carries energy and momentum into the surrounding gas. Turn the wind up and watch the
star's track shift and its future remnant shrink.

## What the reader does

Toggle **winds on/off** and dial the mass-loss rate; watch the star lose mass, its HRD
track respond, and the wind's mechanical luminosity (~½ Ṁv²) accumulate — the fuel for
the feedback chapter.

## The research, woven in

- Wind mass loss reshapes the evolutionary track and the remnant — data from **startrax**.
