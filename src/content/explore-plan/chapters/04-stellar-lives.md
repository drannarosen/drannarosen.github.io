The camera zooms in. We stop looking at the crowd and follow **one star** — by default
the most massive one the reader birthed — through its whole life. (First appearance of
the nested "one star ★" through-line.)

## Story beat

A star is a fusion reactor held together by its own weight. Pick a mass and watch it
trace a **track across the HR diagram** — steady on the main sequence, then swelling off
it into a giant. Mass sets everything: brighter, hotter, and far shorter-lived the
heavier it is. A 20 M⊙ star lives a few Myr; a red dwarf outlives the universe.

## What the reader does

Choose a mass; scrub time; watch the star move on the HRD and its properties change. The
massive star you're following is on a countdown.

## The research, woven in

- The tracks are **precomputed from startrax** (Hurley 2000 SSE), read in the browser as
  data — the exact pattern the validated ZAMS core already established. startrax stays
  canonical; the page interpolates its output.

## Physics & data

Precomputed startrax evolutionary tracks (L, Teff, phase vs age) across a mass grid at
solar Z; browser interpolation. Surrogate fits are a later option.
