The turn where the series bites its own tail — and where Anna's paper becomes the plot.

## Story beat

Everything so far assumed you could see each star cleanly. You can't. Most massive stars
have **unresolved binary companions**, so two stars blur into one brighter, redder point;
**rotation** smears temperatures and ages. Re-observe the very cluster you built and the
census you trusted in Chapters 2–3 is quietly corrupted.

## What the reader does

Toggle **binaries on/off** and add rotation; re-derive the IMF slope from the *observed*
cluster and watch it drift away from the truth you set — the more stars you add, the more
*precisely wrong* it gets.

## The research, woven in

- This is **Confidently Wrong**, made playable: ignoring binaries biases IMF inference,
  and because statistical error shrinks with sample size while the bias does not, huge
  surveys report slopes that are precise **yet wrong**.
- Spin-off: **binary evolution → gravitational-wave mergers** — binaries as villain *and*
  hero (the black-hole pairs LIGO hears).

## Physics & data

Unresolved-binary photometric blending + a rotation prior layered on the sampled cluster;
the naive vs binary-aware IMF fit.

## Carries forward

A **corrupted observation** — the loaded gun for the finale.

Binaries on/off; binary fraction; rotation on/off.
