The turn where the clean single-star picture breaks — twice over. Binaries and rotation
are not just an observational nuisance; they change the **physics** of how stars evolve.
That dual nature is exactly why this chapter sits *inside* the evolution arc, before
feedback, rather than being demoted to an observation-only bias.

## Story beat

Everything so far treated each star as a lone, non-rotating point. Reality: most massive
stars have a **binary companion**, and stars **rotate**. Both change what a star *is* —
mass transfer and tides reroute evolution, rotation enlarges cores and extends lifetimes —
*and* what a star *appears to be*: an unresolved pair blurs into one brighter, redder point.

## What the reader does

Toggle **binaries on/off** and **rotation on/off** on the same cluster. On the theory
face, watch tracks reroute (a mass-gainer rejuvenates; a stripped star bares its core). The
*observational* half — two stars read as one, the inferred IMF drifting **precisely wrong**
as you add stars — is teed up here but paid off in the observation act, where the
**"telescope adds ten pounds"** example returns as a **Confidently Wrong** instance once
`observe()` exists.

## The research, woven in

- Binaries and rotation are first-class evolution physics, not corrections — the honest
  reason this chapter is here and not appended to the finale.
- **Confidently Wrong** seeded: ignoring binaries biases IMF inference, and because
  statistical error shrinks with sample size while the bias does not, huge surveys report
  slopes that are precise **yet wrong**. Fully playable later against `observe()`.
- Deep dive: **binary evolution → gravitational-wave mergers** — binaries as villain *and*
  hero (the black-hole pairs LIGO hears).

## Physics & data

Binary demographics + Eggleton Roche-lobe geometry; a rotation prior on tracks. The
observation-bias half (unresolved blending, naive vs binary-aware IMF fit) is deferred to
the observation act with the rest of `observe()`.

## Carries forward

Rerouted stellar tracks — and a **loaded gun** for the finale's inference reckoning.
