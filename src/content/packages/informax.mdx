Any inference method will return an answer. The harder question is whether the
data ever constrained it, and the usual failure is a confident posterior for
something the observation could not have measured.

`informax` works out what an observation can recover before it is taken, and
attaches a machine-checked verdict to every result, so an analysis can abstain
rather than report false precision. In its own head-to-head test of two design
strategies the gates abstained on both rather than declaring a winner, which is
the behaviour they exist for.

So far that is demonstrated on problems with known truth rather than on real
catalogues.
