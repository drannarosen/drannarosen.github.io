A survey does not measure a population; it measures whatever its selection let
through. `fluxax` puts those effects — magnitude limits, crowding, blending,
footprint, extinction, the parallax zero-point — inside a single differentiable
likelihood, where each one can be shown to bias a recovered IMF slope when it is
ignored, and to come back out when it is modelled.

The error models are checked against the published ones and against the official
instrument curves, not against sky data. Every demonstration here uses synthetic
populations with known answers; fitting real catalogues is
[informax](/software/informax)'s job.
